Dance animation
What is this project about?

In this guide, we'll make a strikingly simple, fun little animation that you can create in a session with complete beginners and little ones alike. Here's the end result:

In the project, the kids first arrange the letters of their own names on the stage, and make them dance. Then they draw a self-portrait, and make it dance, too. Finally, music downloaded from YouTube completes the attraction.

Step 1

First of all, choose the initial letter of your name. There are letters among the Scratch sprites, you can choose from them clicking the “Letters” button at the top:

You can color the chosen letter in the graphic editor, or even bring it to life by adding arms and legs:

Step 2

Now for the other letters. If you like, you can draw them yourself:

This was done, for example, by clicking into an ellipse with an eraser set to very large , reshaping it so that it has an accent, and finally filling it with a color gradient.

Or you can click on the icon and use the graphical font editor tool, choosing the font you find the coolest:

Finally, you can decide not to use the letter itself, but an object similar to the letter, like we did with the lightning bolt turned vertically:

Whatever choose, you'll soon see kids’ names on Scratch stages made up of customized letters:

It is important that each letter is a separate sprite, otherwise we cannot move them separately. Check that the letters of the name are also separated on the thumbnail images below the stage:

If incidentally two or more letters have been added to the same sprite, or even worse, to the background, you can help by creating a new sprite and then copy-pasting it.

Step 3

It's time to move the first letter. There are lots of ways to do this, but spinning is very popular with kids. The code for this is well known from the introductory sessions:

If you want, you can also add a back-and-forth spin to the sprite's movement:

You can even leave it up to the children to animate the following letters, if they already have some experience. For example, one can slide back and forth:

Step 5

If you've made it this far, you'll have a sense of success. Choose a background for your project and start sharing and liking.

But if you feel like continuing, the best is yet to come.

Step 6

Now include yourselves in the project. No need to complicate the drawing; in vector mode, you can draw a few rectangles and circles to make a great human figure.

You can add a dress made of reshaped rectangles, a face with a few lines, and hair made of either lines or ellipses. The drawing is not meant to look exactly like you, so take it easy and use your imagination! :)

Step 7

Now we'll make the newly drawn figure dance. First of all, we need four copies of his costume. To duplicate, right-click on the thumbnail of the costume and in the pop-up menu, click on duplicate:

The second and the fourth picture will be edited by tilting the figure’s arms, legs, and head first one way, and then the opposite way. No need to aim for perfection here either! :) But if something really goes wrong, feel free to make more duplicates of the original costume.

Finally, the code. It's very simple, since we only need to change costumes continuously, with a small delay so that the movement is not too fast.

At this point, you can once again complete the project successfully, but if you still have the stamina, you can make it perfect with a last few minutes of effort.

Step 8

The last step is to add music to the dance. Search Youtube for your favorite music. You don't have to listen to it all, you just need the link to the video.

In the address bar of your browser, mark and copy the link (url) to the video: . Some of the participants may not know what the browser is and where the address bar is, so take the time to do this.

There are many online web apps for downloading youtube music; unfortunately, not all of them work reliably and you can never be sure that they will be gone by next week. At the time of writing this lesson, https://mp3-convert.org/ was stable. Before the session, it is worth testing to see if it is still working and finding another one if necessary.

Open the download page, copy and paste the link to the youtube video into the search box and click "Convert".

The conversion will take a minute or two; then click on the "Download" button to download the mp3 file.

Now go back to Scratch and click on "Sounds" next to the "Costumes" tab for the last sprite you drew: . Select the upload option at the bottom left. The file will probably be in your "Downloads" folder, depending on your system and browser settings. (If it's not there, run a search or check your browser settings.) After a successful upload, the file will appear in your sounds.
