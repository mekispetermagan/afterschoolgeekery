Using Lego Spike Prime: Getting Started
What is this guide about?
This quide will provide you a detailed explanation of the first steps to get your robotics sets working, so that you get ready to start any of the project works with the kids.
1. Building

Each yellow box contains one full set of lego parts for building various robots. There are a few special parts.

Hub: This is a minicomputer. It is the brain and heart of the robot.It receives information from the sensors, makes decisions based on the information, and moves the motors accordingly. It also contains the battery.

Motors: There are one large and two small motors in the set. These can move various parts of your robot: turn wheels, raise an arm, turn the head, rotate the propeller, wag the tail, etc.

Sensors: the distance sensor looks like a pair of eyes, and it detects how close an object is. The color sensor looks like a lamp, and it detects the main color of an object in front of it. The touch sensor looks like a button, and it is sensitive to pressure. All sensors report the information they detect to the hub.

The rest of the set consists mainly of larger bits with holes, and small bits that fit in the holes and connect the larger bits.

2. Setting up the app

You can program your robots offline (from your computers, without internet) or online (using a web app). In both cases, start opening this website in the Chrome browser.

To get the offline app, just click Download, and install the downloaded file. To work online, click on the downward arrow on the right, and click on “Launch web app”.

The offline and the online apps have the same interface. Here we present the web app, but everything is just about the same in the offline app as well.

First, select Spike Prime.

Confirm that you have a set to connect.

Now click on “New project” (not “Start”).

Select “Word blocks”. This will be practically a version of Scratch.

3. Connecting the hub

To connect your robot to the app, click on the yellow circle.

Switch on your robot by pressing the large button on the hub. Press the small button with the bluetooth symbol. Select the color of your button (green or white).

If you see an "open" button, click on it.

If this is the first time to connect your hub, bluetooth connection may not be possible until you update the hub. If this is the case, the appearing list of compatible devices will be empty.

If this is the case, close the list and select the USB option instead of bluetooth.

Use the white USB cable to connect the hub to your computer. Now the list will not be empty.

At first connection you will either be prompted to update your hub, or the update will start automatically.

After update you can name your robot as you like.

After updating you can connect your hub either via blootooth or via USB.

4. Programming the robot

When the hub is successfully connected to your computer, the yellow circle in the app turns white with a green check symbol, and each motor and sensor is indicated with an icon, along with the letter of the port they are plugged in.

Now you can start putting together your code. Use the yellow “event” blocks for the sensors, and the blue “motion” blocks for themotors. The rest is just plain Scratch.
