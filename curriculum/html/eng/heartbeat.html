Heartbeat
What is this project about?

This is a very simple animation that can be made in up to 20 minutes. It's a completely beginner level project, you can even take it to your first session. A heart beats on the stage and changes color. You can see the final result here:

Step 1: Choosing the sprite

Say goodbye to Scratchie the cat, he won't be needed now. Click on the blue cat head icon in the bottom right to open the spite library, and choose your favorite one of the three heart sprites:

Also choose a background to your liking and place the heart sprite on it:

Step 2: Coding

The code for the beating will set the size of the heart varyingly; sometimes larger, sometimes smaller. So, when you click on the green flag, you set the size to a larger value (two hundred if you want a big heart, twenty if you want a very small one), and then after a little delay, a bit smaller (again, to each their liking):

To keep it beating, you can put the code in a "forever" block with another delay:

The heart is beating nicely, but very slowly. Before you set the exact values to expect, discuss how fast the heart beats in a minute. You can even measure your pulse by tapping your wrists. The number will be somewhere around 80 in 60 seconds, so a heartbeat lasts a little less than a second. But even that has to be split into two. How? What rhythm does the heart beat at? You can even make a chorus of the rhythm out loud:

Ba-bump, ba-bump, ba-bump
--one short, one long. Set the values accordingly, for example 0.2 and 0.6 seconds (be careful, Scratch uses decimal points instead of the decimal comma you might have learned in school):
