Drawing with the Scratch editor

Introduction
What is this guide about?

This guide provides an introduction to creating vector graphics in the Scratch graphics editor. The vector graphics tools are simple to use and make drawing easy for those without good manual dexterity. You can find the images created in this guide in this Scratch project:

The Graphic editor

The first section introduces you to Scratch's graphical editor. The editor can be used in two different modes; we will only focus on the vector mode.

Drawing with simple shapes

It is surprising how much can be drawn with solid circles, rectangles and lines. In the second section, we will practice this.

Drawing with complex shapes

Once you're comfortable with solid, geometric shapes, you can start to color and reshape them with color transitions (gradients) to get more life-like shapes. In the third section, we'll delve into this.

Drawing patterns

Once you're comfortable with complex shapes, you can start copying them and arranging them in patterns to create detailed drawings with little work. The fourth section is devoted to this.

Challenges

Finally, in the last section we give you some challenges to practice what you have learned.

The Graphic editor
What is it?

You can open the graphic editor by clicking on the "Costumes" tab at the top left: . The editor always edits the costumes of the selected sprite. And if the stage is selected, you can edit backgrounds, and the tab caption will change: .

If your sprite has multiple costumes, you can select the costume you want to edit in the bar on the left:

Here you can also choose a new costume from the costume, library, create a blank costume or upload an image file from your own computer:

Vector image vs bitmap image

With Scratch's graphical editor, you can create two types of drawings:

  • bitmap: this consists of pixels (like when you work with Paint, Gimp, or Photoshop)
  • vector image: this consists of geometric shapes (like when you work with SVG Editor, Inkscape, or Illustrator)
You can switch between the two modes using the "Convert" button: / You should use this with caution, as converting will result in data loss. Except in some rare cases, images started in vector graphics mode should be kept as vector graphics.

Toolbars

The graphical editor has two toolbars; these are located on the left side of the image and above the image. The content of the left toolbar is constant, while the tools on the top change depending on which tool you select on the left.

These toolbars will be explained in more detail in the following sections, but you can explore them now. If you don't like the results, just click the undo tool:

Objects

Vector images are made up of separate, often overlapping, colored objects that can be individually selected, resized and moved. Test how many objects make up the cat's costume by clicking on them with your mouse (tip: you can use the "Ungroup" tool to break up the selected composite objects.)

When you're done, undo your changes, because in the next section we'll work with the cat... :)

Drawing with simple shapes
What will happen?

Start by dressing up Scratchie the cat with colored rectangles, ellipses (elongated circles), and lines.

Step 1

Let's start with the color editor. Find your desired color by adjusting the three color sliders.

Step 2

Left-click on the circle tool and drag the mouse slightly diagonally to the right on the drawing surface to get a nice elongated ellipse.

Step 3

Now click on the selection tool at the left: , and drag the ellipse onto the kitten's forehead.

Step 4

Draw another, less elongated ellipse and draw it over the previous one. Lo and behold, in just a few moves we've made him a pretty decent cap!

If the dimensions are wrong or an object is out of place, select it again, and adjust it until you're happy with the result. We're not in a hurry! :)

Step 5

If you prefer hats, you can start drawing with the rectangle tool: .

Step 6

If you want to make the hat more stylish, tilt it by holding the curved arrow below the selection: .

Step 7

If you change your mind about a color, click the fill tool: , choose another color from the palette, and click to recolor your objects.

Step 9

You can also change the fill color to transparency by clicking on the crossed out square icon: , and even change the outline color and thickness: . o you can even add glasses to the kitten using the circle tool and the line tool ().

Step 10

And so on: with coloured ellipses, rectangles and lines, you can dress up Scratchie cat however you like. Add as many details and make as many versions as you like! :)

Drawing with complex shapes
Step 1

Let’s dress up Scratchie once more, but this time give him a crown. It would be difficult to make this shape out of ellipses and rectangles. But an ellipse will do for a start:

Step 2

Now click on the reshape icon next to the selection tool: . If you then click on the ellipse, you will see its outline in blue with four control points. You can move these to reshape the ellipse.

Step 3

You can also click on the outline to insert new control points. If you want a selected point to be pointed or curved, click on the corresponding icon: . As the points multiply, the crown begins to take shape.

Step 4

Keep adding and adjusting the dots until you have a pretty little crown.

Step 5

Now we give it a little glamour with the Fill tool: . At the top of the palette, there are four options for a gradient: . As you can guess from their shades, these are:

  • no color gradient
  • horizontal color gradient
  • vertical color gradient
  • central color gradient

If you choose the central color gradient, set the middle color to white and the outer color to yellow, then click on the center of the crown to give it a little shine.

Step 6

The crown comes with a robe! You can also start this one with colored ellipses.

As our drawing skills develop, so does our imagination; we can now easily imagine the finished robe in rudimentary shapes.

Step 7

Reshape the robe to suit your ideas as much as possible.

Don't be discouraged if something doesn't work the first time. Undo the last step, and try again until you are satisfied with the result. The goal is not perfection, but to experience how easy it is to draw like this!

Step 8

Finally, you can use the back and forth tools to place the robe in front or behind Scratchie:

Step 9

And so on: add as many elements as you like to your drawing to create the character you want.

Patterns: repetition and symmetry
Step 1

Sometimes it's good not to have to keep redrawing the repetitive details of an image. For example, to draw a flower, you only need to work on a single petal.

Draw a heart shape for the petal. It doesn't have to be completely pointed in the middle at the top, just use your mouse to make the guides a little shorter.

Step 2

Use the fill tool and your favorite colors to give it a nice gradient. And you can remove the outline by setting the line width to zero.

Step 3

Now select the petal and click on the copy and paste icon at the top: . If it's easier, you can also copy with the usual Ctrl+C and Ctrl+V (Cmd+C and Cmd+V if you have a Mac).

Step 4

Tilt the copy quite slightly and place it close to the original.

Step 5

Repeat this until you get around the circle.

Step 6

If you want your flowers to be perfectly symmetrical, click on the eraser tool: , and erase the last two petals before you put them in place. This is a bit more difficult, feel free to skip it if you don't want to fiddle with it.

Step 7

Add a colorful circle in the middle, and you have a flower.

Select the whole image using the mouse or Ctrl+A, then click the grouping tool to avoid it falling apart during subsequent edits.

Step 8

Now you can start copying, pasting, resizing and rotating the flower as you like.

Step 9

Now it’s time to draw the leaves. It is enough to draw one of these in the usual way, starting from an ellipse, reshaping it and finally filling it in with a color transition.

Step 10

Now the leaf can be reproduced. Besides rotating and resizing, you can also mirror one or two of them by clicking on this icon: . With the flowers and leaves, the whole plant takes shape.

Step 11

If you want to include stems, use the line tool to draw them and the reshape tool to bend them in the middle.

Step 12

Finally, click on the stage on the right edge: , and use two gradient rectangles to create a background for your plant.

Here we go! It was easy, wasn't it? :)
